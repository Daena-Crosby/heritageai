Anansi and the Wisdom Pot

history_edu West African & Caribbean Heritage

Oral Tradition

Narrated by Elder Brown

Original Patois

"Anansi did tink seh him smart pass anybody else pan de earth. Him decide seh him gwine collect all de wisdom inna de world and lock it up inna one pot so dat nobody else can have none..."

English Translation

Anansi thought that he was wiser than anyone else on the earth. He decided that he was going to collect all the wisdom in the world and lock it up in a pot so that no one else could have any...

local_library Cultural Lexicon

Smart pass

A Patois expression meaning "smarter than" or "to outsmart someone." It highlights the comparative nature of wit in folklore.

Gwine

Derived from "going to." In Jamaican Patois, it indicates future intent or an action that is about to occur.

Tink seh

Meaning "think that." Used to introduce a thought, belief, or perceived reality.

Inna

A common contraction for "in a" or "into a," used frequently for spatial positioning in narrative.
