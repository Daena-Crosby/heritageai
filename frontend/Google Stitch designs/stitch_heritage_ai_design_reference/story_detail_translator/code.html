Cultural Context

"A story about the futility of greed and the communal nature of wisdom. Originally from the Ashanti people, preserved through the diaspora."

Original Patois

"Once upon a time, Anansi de spider did tink him smart pass everybody. Him decide say him gwine gather up all de wisdom inna de world and lock it up inna one big clay pot..."

Him search high, him search low. Him go through de valley and climb up de hill. Every likkle piece a wisdom him find, him pop it inna de pot. When him finish, de pot did full to de brim. Anansi never waan share it wid nobody, so him decide fi hide it pon top a de tallest silk cotton tree inna de forest.

English Translation

"Long ago, Anansi the spider believed he was cleverer than anyone else. He decided he would gather all the wisdom in the world and lock it away inside a large clay pot..."

He searched high and low. He traveled through valleys and climbed mountains. Every bit of wisdom he found, he placed it inside the pot. When he was finished, the pot was full to the brim. Anansi didn't want to share it with anyone, so he decided to hide it at the top of the tallest silk cotton tree in the forest.

The Wisdom Pot Artifact

In many versions of this tale, the 'pot' represents the heart. The story reminds us that knowledge is heavy when kept for oneself, but light when shared with the tribe.
